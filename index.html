Mauvais Ordre

Released on September 16, 2022, Mauvais Ordre is Lomepal's third studio album. Unveiled on June 3, 2022, "Tee" is the first single, marking the rapper's return to the forefront of the musical scene, more than two and a half years after the release of Amina, the reissue of his second studio album Jeannine. A few weeks later, on June 30, 2022, Le Parisien unveiled "Auburn", the second single from the album, accompanied by a video featuring dancer Gal Zusmanovich. On August 28, 2022, in a live broadcast on his YouTube channel filming the Boulevard de Clichy at the Blanche metro station in Paris, the rapper announced the name, release date and cover art of this third album - by posting them on a wall of the main thoroughfare - as well as a tour to promote its release. The announcement was accompanied by a dedicated pre-order site and a tracklist of fifteen tracks, with no collaborations. In just six days, the project is certified gold, with over 50,000 cumulative sales across France. In mid-February 2023, it was certified double platinum.

Jeanine

Jeannine, Lomepal's second studio album, was released on December 7, 2018. The album's lead single, "1000°C," featuring Belgian rapper Roméo Elvis, was released on September 17, 2018, along with a music video. Produced over nine months, Jeannine includes collaborations with Roméo Elvis, Orelsan, JeanJass, and Katerine. The album features contributions from six composers: Superpoze, Pierrick Devin, VM The Don, Stwo, Mohave, Vladimir Cauchemar, and Antoine himself on select tracks. The title track is a tribute to Lomepal's maternal grandmother, Jeannine Rose Pambrun, who passed away in 2000. Pascale Valentinelli, Antoine's mother, shares stories about Jeannine throughout the album. The cover features Jeannine's eyes from a 1960s photograph. Jeannine's first-week sales in France reached 39,332 copies, achieving gold status in ten days, platinum in four weeks, and triple platinum by November 2019, with over 300,000 sales. The album also achieved gold status in Belgium. A reissue, Amina, was released on October 25, 2019, shortly after reaching triple platinum status.

Lomepal

Antoine Valentinelli, known as Lomepal, is a French rapper and singer, born on December 4, 1991 in the 13th arrondissement of Paris.

After his rap debut in 2011, Lomepal released five EPs: Le Singe Fume Sa Cigarette in 2013, in collaboration with Caballero and Hologram Lo' , Cette foutue perle in 2013, Seigneur in 2014, Majesté in 2015 and ODSL in 2016; then three studio albums: FLIP, released in June 2017 and certified triple platinum1, Jeannine, released in December 2018 is certified diamond disc (reissue released in 2019 under the title Amina), and Mauvais ordre, released in September 20222.

At the same time, he befriended other rappers such as Belgian artists Caballero and JeanJass, as well as Roméo Elvis and Fixpen Sill. By 2017, Lomepal had already racked up over a hundred concerts and 15 million views on his YouTube channel3.

The Parisian rapper owes his stage name to his childhood friends. These friends always thought he was sick because of his pale complexion, hence Lomepal, a phonetic transcription of "pale man". His first stage name was Jo Pump4.
